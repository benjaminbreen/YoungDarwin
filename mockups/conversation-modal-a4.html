Field dispatch 18 September 1835   /   recorded privately

Syms kneels beside the gull, looking from its damaged wing to the open specimen bag.

“It is a handsome bird, sir. A shame about the wing. I have kept the feathers clear of the sand, but it will not travel well unless we make a decision.”

“Would you like it labelled now, or should I leave it out for your notes?”
